# 🎸 Guitar Practice — Pentatonic, Blues & Fingerpicking

> Goal: Learn scales + fingerpicking/lead for songwriting
> Level: Intermediate (knows chord shapes, limited scale knowledge)
> Format: 10–15 min sessions, sequential

---

## Week 1 — A Minor Pentatonic Foundation

### Session 1 — A Minor Pentatonic Position 1

Learn the A minor pentatonic scale — position 1 (5th fret). Play ascending and descending at 60 BPM. 4 clean runs without mistakes.

```
e|-------------------5--8--|
B|--------------5--8-------|
G|---------5--7------------|
D|----5--7-----------------|
A|----5--7-----------------|
E|5--8---------------------|
```

### Session 4 — Connect Position 1 → Position 2

Play position 1 ascending, slide from 8th fret low E into position 2, continue ascending. Come back down through position 2 into position 1. 50 BPM.

```
Position 1                         Position 2
e|-----------5--8--|                e|--------------------8--10--|
B|------5--8-------|                B|---------------8--10-------|
G|----5--7---------|  --slide-->    G|----------7--9-------------|
D|----5--7---------|                D|-----7--9-----------------|
A|----5--7---------|                A|-----7--10----------------|
E|5--8-------------|                E|7--8----------------------|
```

Slide on the low E string from fret 8 (end of position 1) to fret 7 (start of position 2).

- [ ] Completed

---

### Session 5 — The Blues Scale (Add the Blue Note)

Add one note to position 1: fret 6 on the A string (the ♭5 / blue note). You now have the A blues scale.

```
e|-------------------5--8--|
B|--------------5--8-------|
G|---------5--7------------|
D|----5--7-----------------|
A|----5--6--7--------------|    ← fret 6 = blue note
E|5--8---------------------|
```

Play up and down. Notice how that single note changes the entire feel.

- [ ] Completed

### Session 7 — Travis Picking on C Chord

Learn the basic Travis picking pattern. Thumb alternates bass notes (5th and 4th string). Index on 2nd string, middle on 1st string.

```
C Chord — Travis Pick Pattern

e|--------0-------0-------0-------0----|
B|-----1-------1-------1-------1-------|
G|--0-------0-------0-------0---------|
D|-----------------------------------------|
A|--3-----------3-----------3-----------3--|  ← Thumb alternates
E|-----------------------------------------|

    T   i   T   m   T   i   T   m

T = Thumb (bass strings, alternating 5th & 4th)
i = Index (2nd string)
m = Middle (1st string)
```

50 BPM. Repeat until the thumb feels automatic — that's the breakthrough moment.

- [ ] Completed
- [ ] Thumb feels automatic? (Y/N)

---

### Session 8 — Travis Picking on G + Chord Changes

Apply Travis picking to G chord. Thumb now alternates 6th and 4th string. Same finger pattern.

```
G Chord — Travis Pick Pattern

e|--------3-------3-------3-------3----|
B|-----0-------0-------0-------0-------|
G|--0-------0-------0-------0---------|
D|-----------------------------------------|
A|-----------------------------------------|
E|--3-----------3-----------3-----------3--|  ← Thumb on 6th string now

    T   i   T   m   T   i   T   m
```

Then alternate: 4 bars C → 4 bars G → repeat. Focus on keeping the thumb steady through the chord change — that's the hard part.

- [ ] Completed
